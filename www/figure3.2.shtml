Figure 3.2 Consequences of inbreeding up to 20 generations. This illustration of inbreeding is based on the same simplifying assumptions described in the legend to Figure 3.1. Points on the solid line indicate the portion of the genome that will be homozygous in any individual animal at each generation. Points on the dashed line indicate the portion of the genome that will be fixed identically in the two animals chosen to generate the following generation of animals.
